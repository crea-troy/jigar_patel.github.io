The Physics Problem
The LHC produces petabytes of collision data. Finding deviations from the Standard Model — signatures of dark matter, supersymmetry, or entirely unexpected new physics — requires algorithms that can characterize known processes with enough precision to detect the unknown. My work builds physics-aware generative models for this challenge.
🧠
The ML Problem
Modern deep learning is systematically overconfident. In a scientific setting, a model that doesn't know what it doesn't know is dangerous. My research focuses on Bayesian neural architectures that propagate honest uncertainty — enabling physicists to trust ML outputs with quantified epistemic bounds, not just confident point predictions.

Precision-Weighted Confidence Propagation (PWCP)
2025 — Ongoing In preparation

A new neural network architecture that propagates calibrated uncertainty in a single forward pass, using Bayesian inverse-variance message passing inspired by Karl Friston's predictive coding framework. Each layer analytically computes precision-weighted belief updates — no Monte Carlo sampling, no ensemble overhead. The architecture achieves the lowest Expected Calibration Error across five baselines on MNIST and CIFAR-10 (5-seed evaluation), at identical computational cost to a standard deterministic network.

Current work extends PWCP as an architectural layer integrated into Transformer-based event-level encoders for LHC jet anomaly detection, providing per-event epistemic uncertainty on the RODEM benchmark.

Bayesian Latent Diffusion for Collider Anomaly Detection
2025 — 2026 Published on arXiv

Physics-informed anomaly detection for LHC jet data using a Bayesian latent diffusion model, developed in collaboration with Prof. Tommaso Dorigo at INFN Padova. The model combines a probabilistic variational encoder with latent-space diffusion dynamics, enforcing mass decorrelation and latent-correlation regularisation via physics-motivated loss terms. Ablation studies confirm complementary roles of each component for training stability and detection power.

Work is actively continuing: extending to comparisons with state-of-the-art approaches and developing a Transformer-based architecture incorporating event-level kinematics with a PWCP layer for per-event epistemic uncertainty on RODEM jet constituents for fully unsupervised new-physics searches.

SNN + Transformer IceCube Neutrino Physics
τ Neutrino Identification in IceCube · Bayesian SNN+Transformer
2025 — Ongoing In preparation

Hybrid Bayesian Spiking Neural Network–Transformer for real-time ντ classification in IceCube HESE (High Energy Starting Events) data. The SNN front-end natively encodes Cherenkov photon arrival times as spike trains, exploiting temporal precision without feature engineering. A Transformer module integrates global spatial correlations across the 5,160-DOM array. Variational inference provides per-event epistemic uncertainty, enabling principled rejection of atmospheric neutrino backgrounds.
