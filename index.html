Who I am
I'm a physicist and ML researcher driven by a specific discomfort: I don't like predictions without honest uncertainty. In physics, an overconfident algorithm can mask a new particle; in AI, it can mislead a decision. So I build things that are honest about what they don't know.

I grew up in India, spent four years teaching physics, then moved to Germany for research — working across detector hardware, rare B-meson decays at Belle II, and eventually finding my home at the intersection of Bayesian deep learning and high-energy physics.
What I work on
My research lives at the LHC — building ML systems that can detect new physics without knowing what it looks like. I develop Bayesian neural architectures that propagate calibrated uncertainty, physics-informed diffusion models for anomaly detection, and generative models for detector optimization.

Currently working on PWCP — a new architecture for single-pass uncertainty quantification — and extending it to jet-level analysis on RODEM benchmarks at INFN Padova.
Background
M.Sc Physics, JGU Mainz (2024) — thesis on rare B decays at Belle II. Earlier M.Sc on SiC nanostructure synthesis by plasma arc at IPR, India. During my time at JGU I worked across five experimental groups: ATLAS HGTD, τ SPECT, JUNO, ADMX, and a polarized proton target.

Currently seeking PhD and fellowship positions — MSCA Doctoral Networks, CERN programs, INFN-affiliated — in Bayesian ML for HEP or related areas.
